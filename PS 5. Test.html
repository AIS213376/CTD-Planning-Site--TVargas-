Reflection

Looking at the first version of the website to be recreated, I was lost at where to start. The site seemed very messy and our client didn't know how to format a page in a more organized way, which meant we had to find ways to make this possible. After several meetings with our client, we all started contemplating on who was the actual audience for the site. We argued it was for children, but then there was the counter-argument that children wouldn't have the time to go to a website for PE updates. We also said it was for teachers, but we thought that teachers with no relation to PE might not be interested. The most safest answer to who the audience was is the parents, but it is debatable as to whether or not parents have the time to visit a site for one class when they have other resources to do so (calendars, school email, etc). One consideration that we all had was that this site served as a portfolio page for our client, who is a teacher, which can help him showcase his skills as a teacher.

Working with Jacky and Archit was a blast. It was confusing at first since we were the only group out of the box – we were working with a google site instead of making a site out of scratch (HTML/CSS). Therefore, we had a lot of problems sorting out our priorities between our planning site and our client's site. I feel like there could be a more effective way to work if we just figured out our priorities earlier on, as we three all felt that there was a lot of time wasted on trying to think of what to do next.

In the future, I will definitely invest more time in planning. I felt that most of this project was spontaneous and regardless of the results I think that it could be much better if I planned ahead. I enjoyed the fact that it was very easy to communicate with my group and my client, which was a relief since I wasn't looking forward to any tension.
