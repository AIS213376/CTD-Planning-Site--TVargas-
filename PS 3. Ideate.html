Flowchart Thoughts
Although the flowchart is very simplistic, it gives a direct idea of what the big picture of the site would be. The client said that he liked the idea of adding a resource center for parents.
This flowchart is a more narrow idea of what the site would contain. The homepage would mostly include an overview (slideshow) to Health Studies, PE Activities, and Current Events. These overviews will be linked to a more detailed explanation where the user can navigate and explore themselves.

Flowchart 1 and Flowchart 2 has been combined to create Flowchart 3. Flowchart 3 added the idea of resources for PE Activities. However, after consulting with Mr. Chiu, we realized that we needed a much more simplistic flowchart (this one is too complex) and it needed to appeal to the audience.

With that in mind, we had a second meeting with the client to discuss who the audience would be. After deducing that the clients would mostly be students and/or teachers, we created a fourth flowchart.

This flowchart addresses the problem of separating the Elementary School from the Early Childhood. It also focuses more on the big idea of the website and removed junk pages.

This will be our chosen flowchart, as it does address the user experience of the website, resources available to students, teachers and parents, and general simplicity for the client to edit in the future.

Wireframe Thoughts
This homepage separates the design thinking cycle and creates an overview of each step.

I initially thought that this would be an effective homepage as it summarizes all my work. However, it leaves no room for my pre-project work, which I think is an important prelude to this project.
